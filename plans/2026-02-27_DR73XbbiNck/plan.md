# Question-Based Discovery & Copy Transformation Plan

**Source:** [Entrepreneurship | Startups | Innovation](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DR73XbbiNck/?igsh=MWx1ZW80c2dubW9qaw==)
**Category:** sales
**Relevance:** 90%
**Total Hours:** 18.5h
**Status:** review

## Summary

Transform our sales approach from telling to asking by creating question-driven discovery calls, website copy, and email sequences that get prospects to articulate their own pain points and sell themselves on our solutions.

## Key Insights

- We could restructure our discovery calls to be 80% questions and 20% presenting - let prospects sell themselves on why they need AI automation
- Apply this by creating a question bank that gets local service businesses to articulate their lead generation pain points instead of us explaining the problems
- We could use the 'deprivation = motivation' principle by having prospects detail all their failed attempts at getting consistent leads before presenting our solution
- Apply this by training our sales team to ask 'What have you tried so far to get more customers?' to amplify their frustration with current methods
- We could create email sequences that pose questions rather than make claims - 'What's costing you more: missing calls or paying for leads that don't convert?'
- Apply this by reframing our website copy from 'We provide AI chatbots' to 'What if you never missed another potential customer again?'

## Swipe Phrases

- The perfect [service provider] says nothing and only asks questions [website headline]
- There's nothing to disagree with [objection handling]
- They're going to believe way more of what they say than what you say [sales training]
- What have you done so far to try and solve this? [discovery call script]
- Motivation is the equal opposite of deprivation [sales psychology]
- The more deprived someone is of something, the more motivated they are to solve it [email copy]
- You want them to say it, not you [ad copy]
- What's the biggest challenge you're trying to fix with your current lead generation? [outreach]
- So you clarify whether they're there [qualification framework]
- The more frustrated you are with missed opportunities, the more ready you are for automation [email]

## Tasks

### 1. Rewrite homepage hero copy using question-based approach
**Priority:** high | **Hours:** 2.0h | **Tools:** claude_code, website

Replace current homepage hero section with question-driven copy that gets prospects thinking about their problems. Change headline to 'What if you never missed another potential customer again?' Add subheadline: 'The biggest challenge most local businesses face isn't getting leads — it's capturing them when they come.' Include CTA button: 'Find Out What You're Missing'

**Deliverables:**
- New homepage hero HTML with question-based headline: 'What if you never missed another potential customer again?' and supporting copy that prompts self-reflection

### 2. Create question-based discovery call script framework
**Priority:** high | **Hours:** 3.0h | **Tools:** ghl, claude_code

Build a structured discovery call script that is 80% questions, 20% presenting. Include questions like: 'What have you done so far to try and solve your lead generation challenges?' 'What's the biggest challenge you're trying to fix with your current lead generation?' 'How many potential customers do you think you're missing each month?' 'What's costing you more: missing calls or paying for leads that don't convert?' Structure script to get prospects to articulate deprivation before presenting solution.

**Deliverables:**
- Complete discovery call script with 15-20 strategic questions organized by phase (problem clarification, past attempts, current frustration, future vision)
- GHL template for call notes using the question framework

### 3. Build question-driven email sequence for lead nurturing
**Priority:** high | **Hours:** 4.0h | **Tools:** ghl, claude_code

Create 7-email nurture sequence where each email poses questions rather than makes claims. Email 1: 'What if the solution to your lead problem was simpler than you think?' Email 2: 'What's costing you more: missing calls or paying for leads that don't convert?' Email 3: 'How many potential customers walked away because no one answered?' Email 4: 'What have you tried so far to capture every lead?' Email 5: 'The more frustrated you are with missed opportunities, the more ready you are for automation — does this sound like you?' Email 6: 'What would an extra 20 customers per month be worth to your business?' Email 7: 'Ready to stop wondering what if?'

**Deliverables:**
- 7-email sequence with question-based subject lines and body copy
- GHL automation workflow that triggers sequence based on lead magnet download

### 4. Transform services page copy from statements to questions
**Priority:** medium | **Hours:** 2.5h | **Tools:** claude_code, website

Rewrite services page to use question-based approach. AI Chatbot section: 'What if every website visitor could get instant answers to their questions?' Lead Generation section: 'What's the biggest challenge you're trying to fix with your current lead generation?' Automation section: 'How much time are you spending on tasks a computer could handle?' Each service description should start with a question that gets prospects thinking about their pain points.

**Deliverables:**
- Rewritten services page with question-based headlines and descriptions for each service offering
- Before/after comparison showing transformation from statements to questions

**Depends on:** Rewrite homepage hero copy using question-based approach

### 5. Create question-based ad copy variations for Meta campaigns
**Priority:** medium | **Hours:** 2.0h | **Tools:** meta_ads, claude_code

Develop Facebook/Instagram ad variations using question-driven approach. Primary ad: 'What if you never missed another potential customer again?' Secondary ads: 'What's costing you more: missing calls or paying for leads that don't convert?' 'How many customers are you losing while you sleep?' 'What have you tried so far to capture every lead?' Each ad should make prospects reflect on their current situation rather than us telling them what we do.

**Deliverables:**
- 5 different ad copy variations with question-based headlines
- Meta Ads campaign setup with A/B testing structure for question-based vs. traditional copy

### 6. Build prospect self-qualification survey automation
**Priority:** medium | **Hours:** 3.5h | **Tools:** ghl, n8n, claude_code

Create automated survey that prospects fill out using strategic questions from discovery framework. Include questions: 'What have you tried so far to solve your lead generation challenges?' 'On a scale of 1-10, how frustrated are you with missing potential customers?' 'What's the biggest challenge you're trying to fix with your current lead generation?' Survey results automatically create GHL contact with qualification score and trigger appropriate follow-up sequence.

**Deliverables:**
- GHL survey form with 8-10 strategic qualification questions
- n8n workflow that scores responses and triggers appropriate follow-up sequence
- Telegram notification system for high-value qualified prospects

**Depends on:** Create question-based discovery call script framework

### 7. Implement question-based objection handling scripts in GHL
**Priority:** low | **Hours:** 1.5h | **Tools:** ghl, claude_code

Create objection handling responses that use questions to let prospects resolve their own concerns. Price objection: 'What do you think it's costing you to miss potential customers each month?' Time objection: 'How much time are you currently spending on manual follow-up?' Trust objection: 'What would need to happen for you to feel confident moving forward?' Store these as quick-response templates in GHL for sales team use.

**Deliverables:**
- GHL template library with 10 question-based objection handling responses
- Sales team training document on using questions to overcome objections

**Depends on:** Create question-based discovery call script framework
