## Summary

Implement Conner Krenos' objection handling framework across sales calls, website copy, and ad campaigns to overcome price resistance using perspective reframing and future-self visualization. Transform 'It's expensive' objections into closed deals by positioning our automation as the cheaper long-term solution.

## Key Insights

- We could use the 'expensive compared to what' reframe when local businesses say our AI automation setup is too expensive - compare our $2K setup to hiring a full-time receptionist at $40K/year
- Apply the 'sticker shock vs logistics' question to separate real budget concerns from price anchoring - ask 'Is this more about the number itself or do you genuinely not have the budget right now?'
- We could implement future-self visualization in our sales calls - ask prospects 'What would the version of you already booking 50+ appointments per month do right now?'
- Use the 'what's more expensive in the long run' framework - position doing nothing as more costly than investing in automation (losing leads daily vs one-time setup cost)
- Apply the commitment escalation technique by asking prospects to rate their confidence in our system before addressing price, making the investment decision about belief rather than money
- We could adapt the 'no one said it would be easy' positioning for local service businesses struggling with lead generation and appointment setting challenges
